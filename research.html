Research Topic

Physics-Guided AI for Engineering Simulation & Design

We integrate physical understanding with reliable AI workflows
to speed up simulations, predict future physical behavior, and guide better design decisions.

Research Overview

Key Components of AI-based Design

1

Simulation

Computer simulations provide physics-consistent data that anchor AI models to real engineering behavior.

2

AI Modeling

AI models learn how physical systems behave by capturing patterns and rules, not just fitting data points.

3

Prediction

Trained AI model forecasts how systems evolve over time, turning simulation knowledge into usable future insight.

4

Design Optimization

AI connects prediction to decision-making by searching for better designs under physical and practical constraints.

Our Core AI Techniques for AI-based Design

1

Data-Independent AI

Prototyping feedback closes the loop so simulations and AI predictions stay grounded in manufacturable hardware.

2

Long-Term Forecasting

Scaled experiments validate surrogate models and highlight edge cases to improve robustness.

3

Uncertainty Quantification

Integrated software pipelines push AI insights into design reviews, control rooms, and digital twins.

4

Generative Design

Continuous monitoring links AI-enabled decisions to measurable performance, closing the improvement loop.
