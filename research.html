Research Topic

Physics-Guided AI for Aerodynamic Simulation & Design

We combine physics priors with trustworthy AI workflows to accelerate flow simulations, deliver reliable spatio-temporal forecasts, and guide data-driven design recommendations.

Key Components of Physics-Guided AI

1

Data Efficiency & Practicality

Multi-fidelity modeling and flexible data policies keep models reliable even when practical datasets are sparse or noisy.

2

Physical Consistency

Architectures and loss functions encode governing laws so predictions remain physically meaningful.

3

Robust Spatio-Temporal Prediction

Stable integrators and long-horizon training reduce error accumulation in dynamic flow fields.

4

Reliable Uncertainty Quantification

Ensemble-based and Bayesian UQ provide risk-aware metrics so engineers can decide with confidence.

Specialization for Aerodynamic Simulation & Design

Our end-to-end framework integrates the four components above across the entire aerodynamic workflow—from data collection and AI modeling to real-time prediction and design recommendation.

1. Flow Simulations

Flexible meshes and multi-fidelity data conversion keep CFD assets reusable for AI pipelines.

2. AI Modeling

Physics-aware operators blend analytical priors with data-driven representations for compact training.

3. Real-time Prediction

Robust time-integration and graph-based solvers deliver trustworthy spatio-temporal forecasts.

4. Aerodynamic Design

Uncertainty-aware design exploration supports optimization and recommendation loops.
